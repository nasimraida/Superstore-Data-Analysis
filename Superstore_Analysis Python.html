Monthly Trends¶

Analyzing monthly data to identify and understand patterns and fluctuations over time.

#monthly data

#sales
monthly_sales = df.groupby(['Year', 'Month'])['Sales'].sum().reset_index()
monthly_sales['Date'] = pd.to_datetime(monthly_sales[['Year', 'Month']].assign(day = 1))

plt.figure(figsize = (12,5))
plt.plot(monthly_sales['Date'], monthly_sales['Sales'])
plt.gca().yaxis.set_major_formatter(FuncFormatter(lambda x, _: f'${x:,.0f}')) 
    #change formatting of revenue

plt.title("Monthly Revenue")
plt.xlabel("Month")
plt.ylabel("Revenue")
plt.grid(alpha = 0.3)
plt.show()
print()

























Revenue generally increased over the four year period, with noticeable dips in Q1 and spikes in Q4 each year. This pattern suggests seasonal fluctuations, with increased demands and purchasing activity during the holiday season followed by a post holiday slowdown.

#profit margin
monthly_margin = df.groupby(['Year', 'Month']).agg({'Sales':'sum', 'Profit':'sum'}).reset_index()
monthly_margin['Date'] = pd.to_datetime(monthly_margin[['Year', 'Month']].assign(day = 1))

monthly_margin['M Profit Margin'] = (monthly_margin['Profit']/monthly_margin['Sales'])*100

plt.figure(figsize = (12,5))
plt.plot(monthly_margin['Date'], monthly_margin['M Profit Margin'])

plt.title("Monthly Profit Margin")
plt.xlabel("Month")
plt.ylabel("Profit Margin (%)")
plt.grid(alpha = 0.3)
plt.show()
print()




























































Profit margin fluctuated throughout the 4 year period but generally stayed within the 10-20% margin. A notable dip occurred betweeen Q4 2014 and Q1 2015 indicating that profitabiliy decreased despite the continued sales. More data would be required to pinpoint the actual reason for the decline, but it may have been caused by more discounting or increased costs.

#rolling 3 month
rolling = df.groupby(['Year', 'Month'])['Sales'].sum().reset_index()
rolling['Date'] = pd.to_datetime(rolling[['Year', 'Month']].assign(day = 1))

rolling['Avg'] = rolling['Sales'].rolling(window = 3).mean()

plt.figure(figsize = (12,5))
plt.plot(rolling['Date'], rolling['Sales'], label = 'Monthly Revenue')
plt.plot(rolling['Date'], rolling['Avg'], label = '3 Month Average', color = "red")
plt.gca().yaxis.set_major_formatter(FuncFormatter(lambda x, _: f'${x:,.0f}')) 

plt.title("Monthly Revenue vs. Rolling Average")
plt.xlabel("Month")
plt.ylabel("Revenue")
plt.legend()
plt.grid(alpha = 0.3)
plt.show()
print()




























































The 3 month rolling average followed the monthly revenue closely and revealed a steady upward trend over time.
